Sustainable Development and Environmental Concerns

Excessive deforestation, indiscriminate hunting of some species, intensive fishing (hake, for example), and land drilling for mining exploitation (oil, coal, and other minerals) are some examples of activities that can cause damage to the planet.

Environmental specialists propose that human activities should be carried out taking into account the needs of future generations. This type of development is called sustainable development.

The World Commission on Environment of the United Nations (UN) defines sustainable development as "development that meets the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs."
