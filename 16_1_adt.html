Biomes of the World

Climate and terrain are of great importance in the formation of biomes.

For example, some mountain ranges are like huge walls interposed in the path of the moist winds coming from the ocean.

When colliding with the mountain, these winds release their moisture on the slope facing the sea, while the other slope is arid.

Likewise, altitude is a determining factor: in the mountains, biomes are distributed in layers, according to altitude.
