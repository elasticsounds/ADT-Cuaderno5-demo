The cities and environmental problems

Large cities often have various problems caused by the management of a limited territory where millions of people live.

Air pollution is a serious problem in some cities.

It is caused by the emission of gases from vehicle transit and industries.

Smog is the haze that remains in the city, causing health problems and creating an urban microclimate characterized by increased temperature.

Intense traffic is also a source of noise pollution.

The permanent movement of the population within the city and between it and neighboring cities creates transportation problems.

Many times, transportation systems are not sufficient, which causes congestion and chaos during peak hours.

A large city consumes significant volumes of water, food, and energy.

Similarly, economic activities and households generate thousands of tons of garbage every day, which the city's public services must collect, treat, and deposit at final disposal sites.

The same happens with sewage, which must be channeled through sanitation systems, which do not always exist or are not sufficient.
