Incredible America

Many centuries ago, travelers and explorers wrote descriptions of nature, although they were not as accurate as those of today's science.

After an exploratory trip in the Caribbean, Melchor de Barros claimed to have seen a region with good land where giant trees grew. Their size was such that it was impossible to reach their tops by shooting arrows, and six men holding hands were needed to surround their gigantic trunks.
Honorius Philoponus, 1621

Walter Raleigh described the inhabitants of America: men with heads lower than their shoulders, feet as large as sunshades, some with rows of teeth in every maxilla, bear bodies, lion’s paws, and scorpion tails.
Conrad Gesner, 1558

Christopher Columbus, in his diary of his first trip to America in 1492, described the American landscape: "The sea calm as a river and the best air in the world... The singing of the birds is such that one would never want to leave, and the flocks of parrots darken the sun."
Juan de Mandeville, 1521
